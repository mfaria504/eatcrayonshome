Our Story

Coloring Outside
the Lines.

We started EatCrayons because we believed great work and honest partnerships shouldn't be mutually exclusive. Five years later, we still believe that.

Meet
Jess &
Matt.

In 2020, amidst the chaos of a global pandemic, Jess and I founded EatCrayons — driven by a vision for more impactful work that actually meant something. We started with minimal funds, no initial offerings, and no clients. Okay fine, our entrepreneurial spirit wavered slightly. But we recovered.

What we found on the other side of that uncertainty was clarity. Larger agencies were consistently letting clients down — not because of a lack of talent, but because of a lack of stakes. They didn't care enough. We did. And it turns out that's a meaningful competitive advantage.

Five years later, we know exactly what a great partnership looks like. The right personality, the right business challenges, and the right opportunity to create a real win for everyone involved — our clients, their clients, and our team. If we don't believe we're the right fit, we'll tell you on the first call.

EatCrayons has evolved into something we're genuinely proud of: a growth engine that combines sharp strategy, modern infrastructure, and hands-on execution. We're not the biggest agency in the room. We're the one that cares most about what happens when the meeting ends.

Matthew Faria
President & Co-founder

The Name

Why EatCrayons?

01

The Anti-Agency Signal

Most growth partners choose names like ScalePath, Velocity, or Ascend. By choosing EatCrayons, we're immediately signaling that we don't care about corporate posturing. It acts as a filter: if a potential client is put off by a name like this, they probably aren't the fast-moving, execution-focused founder we want to work with anyway.

02

Radical Simplicity

There's a kindergarten-level honesty in the name. While other agencies hide behind complex slide decks and marketing jargon, we focus on the fundamental work that actually moves the needle — like a kid who just wants to create without overthinking the "theory" of art.

03

The Chaotic Creative Edge

Eating crayons is a classic trope for someone who's a bit different — who doesn't follow the instruction manual. We experiment aggressively and fail fast so our clients don't have to. The name leans into that. We're willing to try the unconventional stuff that "smarter" agencies are too stiff to touch.

04

Directness Over Fluff

We don't ask you to fill out a brief and wait two weeks for a deck. We build a roadmap we own from day one. EatCrayons is shorthand for: we aren't here to look smart; we're here to work. It trades professional vanity for raw execution.

In short: we'd rather be the "unprofessional" team that delivers results than the "professional" team that delivers excuses.
