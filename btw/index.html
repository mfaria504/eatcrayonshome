Four principles

Four things we
don't compromise on.

01

Practitioners only

Everyone in the room is operating something real. No consultants selling to the room, no spectators. The currency is direct experience — what you've personally tried, measured, and learned.

02

The present tense

"This week" is load-bearing language. The conversation is always anchored to what's happening now — not theory, not case studies from five years ago. What are you actually doing right now?

03

Compounding trust

The value of any single meeting is real but bounded. The compounding value — shared context, pattern recognition that builds over months — is where Better This Week becomes irreplaceable.

04

Earned exclusivity

The format

One hour.
Structured to produce.

10 min

Opening round

Each member states what's live for them this week in 60 seconds. No backstory, no context-setting. Just the current situation. This surfaces the raw material for the session.

5 min

Thread selection

The room identifies which two or three threads are most generative. Done collaboratively — not by the host. The room owns its agenda.

35 min

Deep work

The selected threads get full attention. This is not a presentation format — it is a dialogue. The goal: leave the person who surfaced the problem with at least one concrete thing they didn't have before.

5 min

Closing round

Each member states one thing they're committing to before next week. One sentence. This creates the accountability loop that makes the weekly cadence meaningful.

A mastermind.Too self-helpy. We trade operational intelligence, not affirmations.
